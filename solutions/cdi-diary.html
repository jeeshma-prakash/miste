Heat stress poses a significant threat to the health, well-being, and productivity of dairy cattle.

As temperatures rise, cows struggle to maintain a comfortable body temperature, leading to a cascade of challenges that can significantly impact your dairy operation:

Reduced Milk Production:

Heat stress disrupts the cow's biological processes, diverting energy away from milk production towards maintaining body temperature. Studies show a potential decrease in milk yield of up to 20%, directly impacting farm profitability.

Compromised Cow Health:

Elevated temperatures can lead to a range of health problems in dairy cattle. Increased panting and sweating can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances. Reduced feed intake weakens the immune system, making cows more susceptible to diseases like mastitis. Heat stress can also negatively impact reproductive performance.

Operational Challenges:

Managing heat stress often requires additional resources and operational changes. Implementing cooling strategies like sprinklers or fans can increase energy consumption. Heat-stressed cows may spend less time grazing, impacting feed utilization and potentially increasing labor costs associated with managing feed intake.
