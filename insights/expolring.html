What Is Parametric Insurance for Livestock Farms?

Parametric insurance is an innovative form of coverage that differs significantly from traditional indemnity insurance. In standard insurance, the payout is based on the assessment of actual loss after a claim is filed, which can take time and requires significant paperwork. On the other hand, parametric insurance is triggered automatically when predefined parameters or indices — such as temperature or rainfall thresholds — are met, without needing a formal loss assessment.

For example, in the case of livestock farms, parametric agricultural insurance could be based on weather conditions like extreme heat or cold, drought, or heavy rainfall that could harm the health and productivity of your livestock. If the parameters are met (e.g., temperature exceeds a certain threshold for a specific period), the insurer provides a payout, regardless of the actual loss suffered. This offers quicker and more transparent claims processes and helps farmers get back on their feet faster.
